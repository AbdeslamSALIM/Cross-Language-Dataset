(2) A substantial benefit to society can be attained if all vehicles are provided with safety belts. (3) In its Resolution of 18 February 1986 on common measures to reduce road accidents, as part of the Community's programme for road safety [3], the European Parliament stressed the need for making the wearing of safety belts compulsory for all passengers, including children, except in public service vehicles.
Amendments to this Directive shall be adopted in accordance with Article 13 of Directive 70/156/EEC.
Member States shall adopt and publish the laws, regulations and administrative provisions necessary to comply with this Directive before 20 April 2006.
They shall apply those measures from 21 April 2006. 3.
Article 5 Entry into force This Directive shall enter into force on the 20th day following its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.
Article 6 Addressees This Directive is addressed to the Member States.
