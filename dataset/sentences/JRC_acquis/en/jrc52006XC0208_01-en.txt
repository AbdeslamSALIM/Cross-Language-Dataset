The natural and salted butter will be produced and packaged exclusively in establishments listed in the register of milk plants.
It will be stored at a temperature below or equal to 5 °C.
During transportation to the milk plants, the temperature of the milk will not be allowed to exceed 9 °C.
A mixture of the lactic cultures Lactococcus lactis lactis, L.
lactis cremoris and L.
Maturation will take place over 12 to 15 hours, at a temperature of 12 to 15 °C, with the cultures added 3-4 hours after commencement of the ripening process.
From there, the cream will be taken to the churn and churned for two to three hours.
The fat will then be kneaded slowly for 60 to 120 minutes in a non-continuous butter churn until the butter is formed.
The garnish will not exceed 15 % of the contents.
