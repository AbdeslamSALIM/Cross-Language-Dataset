Article 2 For the purposes of this Directive: (a) %quot%commercial vehicle%quot% shall mean those motor vehicles and trailers defined in categories 1, 2 and 3 of Annex I to Directive 96/96/EC; (b) %quot%technical roadside inspection%quot% shall mean an inspection of a technical nature, not announced by the authorities and therefore unexpected, of a commercial vehicle circulating within the territory of a Member State carried out on the public highway by the authorities, or under their supervision; (c) %quot%roadworthiness test%quot% shall mean a test of a vehicle's technical roadworthiness as provided for in Annex II to Directive 96/96/EC.
A specimen report is contained in Annex I, point 10 of which contains a checklist.
If the authority or the inspector considers that deficiencies in the maintenance of a commercial vehicle may represent a safety risk such that, as regards the brakes in particular, further examination is justified, the commercial vehicle may be subjected to a more elaborate test at a testing centre in the vicinity, designated by the Member State, in accordance with Article 2 of Directive 96/96/EC.
The first data submitted shall cover a period of two years beginning on 1 January 2003.
The Commission shall be assisted by the Committee on the Adaptation to Technical Progress set up pursuant to Article 8 of Directive 96/96/EC, hereinafter referred to as %quot%the Committee%quot%. 2.

Article 10 Member States shall draw up arrangements for the penalties applicable where a driver or operator fails to abide by the technical requirements verified on the basis of this Directive.
Article 13 This Directive shall enter into force on the day of its publication in the Official Journal of the European Communities.
Article 14 This Directive is addressed to the Member States.
