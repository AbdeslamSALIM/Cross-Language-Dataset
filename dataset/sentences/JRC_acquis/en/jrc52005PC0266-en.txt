Proposal for a Regulation of the European Parliament and of the Council establishing accompanying measures for Sugar Protocol countries affected by the reform of the EU sugar regime {SEC(2005) 828} /* COM/2005/0266 final - COD 2005/0117 */ [pic] | COMMISSION OF THE EUROPEAN COMMUNITIES | Brussels, 22.6.2005 COM(2005) 266 final 2005/0117 (COD) Proposal for a REGULATION OF THE EUROPEAN PARLIAMENT AND OF THE COUNCIL establishing accompanying measures for Sugar Protocol countries affected by the reform of the EU sugar regime (presented by the Commission) {SEC(2005) 828} EXPLANATORY MEMORANDUM The EU sugar reform, with its proposed price cuts, may lead to significant impacts and adjustment needs, with broad socio-economic consequences in ACP countries that are signatories to the Sugar Protocol and have been relying on preferential sugar exports to the EU, in most cases since 1975.
The countries to be covered by this scheme are the 18 ACP countries that are signatories to the Sugar Protocol and export sugar at present to the EU.
In 2006, the total budget for the assistance scheme needs to allow the 18 Sugar Protocol countries to undertake priority investment and programmes in the framework of the above-mentioned adaptation strategy.
Article 2 Definitions For the purpose of this Regulation: - « Sugar Protocol countries » means the ACP countries listed in Annex I, - « sugar » means raw or white cane sugar.
Article 3 Eligibility for assistance; procedure 1.
Requests for financial and technical assistance shall be presented no later than 60 days after the entry into force of this regulation. 3.
The requests shall be based on a comprehensive multi-annual adaptation strategy, defined by the country concerned in accordance with Article 4, in consultation with all stakeholders. 4.
Sugar Protocol countries which table a request, which is not based on a comprehensive, multi-annual adaptation strategy, shall only be eligible in 2006 to financial and technical assistance aiming to contribute to the elaboration of such a strategy.
Article 4 Multi-annual adaptation strategy 1.
Article 5 Measures adopted by the Commission 1.
After consultation with the Sugar Protocol country, the multi-annual adaptation strategy shall be adopted under the procedure referred to in Article 7(2), and in accordance with Article 4. 2.
For countries finding themselves in a political crisis, unrelated to the evolution of the sugar sector, the delivery of assistance under this Regulation will be assessed by the Commission on a case by case basis. 3.
Assistance to Sugar Protocol countries without a multi-annual adaptation strategy shall be subject in 2006 to an annual work programme, adopted under the procedure referred to in Article 7(2). 4.
Article 6 Implementation of measures The measures financed under this Regulation shall be implemented in accordance with general rules as set out in Council Regulation (EC) No 1605/20029, namely the Financial Regulation applicable to the general budget of the European Communities.
Article 7 Committee procedure 1.
The period laid down in Article 4(3) of Decision 1999/468/EC shall be set at 30 days. 3.
Article 8 Overall amount The financial reference amount for implementation of this Regulation for 2006 is EUR 40 million.
Article 9 Allocation of overall amount 1.
The financial reference amount for the implementation of the financial and technical assistance referred to in Article 3(4), aiming to contribute to the elaboration of a multi-annual strategy, is EUR 300 000. 4.
Article 10 Protection of the financial interests of the Community 1.
They shall also expressly authorise the Commission to carry out on-the-spot checks and inspections, as provided for in Regulation (Euratom, EC) No 2185/96. 3.
All contracts resulting from the implementation of assistance shall ensure the rights of the Commission and the Court of Auditors under paragraph 2 during and after the performance of the contracts.
Article 11 Period of validity 1.
It shall apply until 31 December 2006.
In the event that the Development Cooperation and Economic Cooperation Instrument has not entered into force on 1 January 2007, the Commission shall be permitted to extend the validity of this Regulation and to adopt further requisite measures.
