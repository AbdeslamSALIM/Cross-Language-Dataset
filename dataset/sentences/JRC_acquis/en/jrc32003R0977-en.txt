A tariff quota for 169000 young male bovine animals covered by CN codes 0102 90 05, 0102 90 29 or 0102 90 49 and intended for fattening in the Community is hereby opened for the period 1 July 2003 to 30 June 2004.
The serial number of the quota shall be 09.4005. 2.
That rate of duty shall apply on condition that the imported animals are fattened for at least 120 days in the Member State which has issued the import licence.
Applications for import rights shall be presented: - in Italy for the quantities referred to in paragraph 1(a), - in Greece for the quantities referred to in paragraph 1(b), - in the Member States of registration for the quantities referred to in paragraph 1(c). 4.
The quantities referred to in paragraph 1(c) shall be allocated upon application to operators who furnish proof that in the period from 1 January 2002 to 31 December 2002 they have exported to or imported from third countries at least 75 live animals covered by CN code 0102 90. 5.
Operators who are no longer engaged in trade in live bovine animals on 1 January 2003 shall not qualify under the arrangements provided for in this Regulation. 2.
Where, under any one category referred to respectively in the first indent of Article 2(2), the second indent of Article 2(2) and Article 2(4) an applicant submits more than one application, all such applications shall be rejected. 2.
Each application under the second indent of Article 2(2) and under Article 2(4) shall relate to a minimum of 50 head and a maximum of 10 % of the available number of head. 4.
As regards applications under Article 2(2), after verification of the documents presented, Italy and Greece shall forward to the Commission by 2 July 2003 at the latest a list of applicants and quantities applied for using the forms set out in Annex II for applications under the first indent of Article 2(2) and in Annex III for applications under the second indent of Article 2(2).
As regards applications under Article 2(4), after verification of the documents presented, Member States shall forward to the Commission by 2 July 2003 at the latest a list of applicants and quantities applied for using the form set out in Annex III.
Regulations (EC) No 1291/2000 and (EC) No 1445/95 shall apply, save as otherwise provided in this Regulation. 3.
Import licences for the remaining quantities shall be issued from 1 December 2003. 5.
However, no licences shall be valid after 30 June 2004. 3.
The security relating to the import licence shall be EUR 20 per head and shall be lodged by the applicant together with the licence application. 4.
Article 8(4) of Regulation (EC) No 1291/2000 shall not apply. 6.
However, where the time limit referred to in the first indent of paragraph 1 has not been observed, the security to be released shall be reduced by: - 15 %, and by - 2 % of the remaining amount for each day by which it has been exceeded.
However, the date of application mentioned in Article 4(2) shall be 27 February 2004 and the date of communication mentioned in Article 4(4) shall be 5 March 2004.
Article 10 This Regulation shall enter into force on the third day following its publication in the Official Journal of the European Union.
