The application shall be accompanied by all available supporting information and documentation as set out in paragraphs 2 to 5. 2.
Details of the trials such as layout, procedure of assessment, and data analysis must be provided; the trials must have been carried out on relevant site types and in accordance with the requirements set out in point 1 of Annex V to Directive 1999/105/EC.
Article 2 As regards applications to prohibit the marketing of forest reproductive material in the categories %quot%source-identified%quot% or %quot%selected%quot% from approved basic material in the form of seed source or stand, the Member State seeking to prohibit the material must provide an assessment of its unsuitability made by reference to the region of provenance.
Article 3 As regards applications to prohibit the marketing of reproductive material in the categories %quot%qualified%quot% or %quot%tested%quot% from approved basic material in the form of seed orchard, parents of family, clone or clonal mixture, the Member State seeking to prohibit the material must provide an assessment of unsuitability made by reference to the individual units of approval.
In addition to the information specified in Article 10 of Directive 1999/105/EC, the following information must be provided on request: (a) the selection criteria used for the components of the basic material; (b) the composition of the basic material; (c) the region(s) of provenance in which the original components were selected.
The Member State referred to in paragraph 2 shall have three months to consider the application and to inform the Commission of its position.
Article 5 This Regulation shall enter into force on the 20th day following its publication in the Official Journal of the European Communities.
It shall apply from 1 January 2003.
