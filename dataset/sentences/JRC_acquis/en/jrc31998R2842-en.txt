Article 5 The Commission shall afford to parties against which objections have been raised the opportunity to develop their arguments at an oral hearing, if they so request in their written comments.
Article 7 Where the Commission raises objections relating to an issue in respect of which it has received an application on a complaint as referred to in Article 6, it shall provide an applicant or complainant with a copy of the non-confidential version of the objections and set a date by which the applicant or complainant may make known its views in writing.
Article 8 The Commission may, where appropriate, afford to applicants and complainants the opportunity of orally expressing their views, if they so request in their written comments.
CHAPTER IV Hearing of other third parties Article 9 1.
The Commission may, where appropriate, invite parties referred to in paragraph 1 to develop their arguments at the oral hearing of the parties against which objections have been raised, if they so request in their written comments. 3.
CHAPTER V General provisions Article 10 Hearings shall be conducted by the Hearing Officer.
CHAPTER VI Final provisions Article 15 1.
Article 16 This Regulation shall enter into force on 1 February 1999.
