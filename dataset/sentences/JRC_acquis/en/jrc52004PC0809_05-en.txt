The “Thessaloniki Agenda for the Western Balkans: moving towards European integration”, endorsed at the European Council on 20 June 2003, confirmed the European prospects of the Western Balkan countries participating in the stabilisation and association process (SAP).
On 5 March 2004 the Commission sent the Council a recommendation for the adoption of negotiating directives, and on 29 April 2004 the Council adopted the decision authorising the Commission to negotiate framework agreements with Albania, Bosnia and Herzegovina, the Former Yugoslav Republic of Macedonia and Serbia and Montenegro on their participation in Community programmes and approving negotiating directives. 4.
The framework agreements have been negotiated in accordance with the negotiating directives annexed to the Council’s decision of 29 April 2004.
