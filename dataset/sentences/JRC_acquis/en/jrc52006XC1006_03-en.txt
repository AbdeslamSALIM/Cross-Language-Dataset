Commission Opinion of 5 October 2006 concerning the plan for the disposal of radioactive waste arising from the dismantling of the Buildings 101A, 102X, 103X, 104X and 153X of the former Eurochemic Reprocessing Plant located in Belgium, in accordance with Article 37 of the Euratom Treaty Commission Opinion of 5 October 2006 concerning the plan for the disposal of radioactive waste arising from the dismantling of the Buildings 101A, 102X, 103X, 104X and 153X of the former Eurochemic Reprocessing Plant located in Belgium, in accordance with Article 37 of the Euratom Treaty (2006/C 241/13) (only the French and the Dutch texts are authentic) On 19 October 2005, the European Commission received from the Belgian Government, in accordance with Article 37 of the Euratom Treaty, General Data relating to the plan for the disposal of radioactive waste arising from the dismantling of the Buildings 101A, 102X, 103X, 104X and 153X of the former Eurochemic Reprocessing Plant.
On the basis of these data and the additional information requested by the Commission on 7 February 2006 and 10 March 2006 and provided by the Belgian authorities on 29 March 2006 and 24 April 2006, and following consultation with the Group of Experts, the Commission has drawn up the following opinion: 1.
The distance between the plant and the nearest point of another Member State, in this case the Netherlands, is approximately 11 km while Germany is at 70 km. 2.
