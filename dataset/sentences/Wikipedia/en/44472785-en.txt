In mathematics, an element x'' of a ring ''R is called nilpotent if there exists some positive integer n'' such that ''xn = 0.ExamplesThis definition can be applied in particular to square matrices.
The matrix A = \begin0&1&0\\ 0&0&1\\ 0&0&0\end is nilpotent because A3 = 0.
