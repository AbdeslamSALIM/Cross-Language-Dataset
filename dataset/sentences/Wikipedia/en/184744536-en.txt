It is also the political center of the country, as seat of government and residence of the Head of State.Lisbon was under Roman rule from 205 BC; Julius Caesar made it a municipium called Felicitas Julia, adding to the name Olissipo.
In return, Olissipo was integrated in the Empire under the name of Felicitas Julia, a Municipium Cives Romanorum.
It was granted self-rule over a territory going as far away as 50 kilometres (30 miles), exempted from taxes, and its citizens given the privileges of Roman citizenship.It was in the newly created province of Lusitania, whose capital was Emerita Augusta.
Snow is extremely rare — Lisbon briefly witnessed snowfalls on January 29, 2006 and January 28, 2007 due to cold waves from the Arctic that affected all Europe at that time.
