Lanzarote has 213&nbsp;km of coastline, of which 10&nbsp;km are sand, 16.5&nbsp;km are beach, and the remainder are rocky.
In the 17th century, pirates raided the island and took 1,000 inhabitants to slavery in Cueva de los Verdes.In 1585, the Ottoman admiral Murat Reis captured Lanzarote.From 1730 to 1736 (for 2,053 days), the island was hit by a series of volcanic eruptions, creating 32 new volcanoes in a stretch of 18 km.
