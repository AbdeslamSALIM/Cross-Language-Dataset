Following this disappointment he travelled to Weimar, where he met Liszt, and Cologne in order to see productions of Richard Wagner's Der Ring des Nibelungen.
In order to support his family Fauré spent most of his time in organising daily services at the Église de la Madeleine and teaching piano and harmony lessons.
He only had time to compose during the summers.
In 1892, he became the inspector of the music conservatories in the French provinces, which meant he no longer had to teach amateur students.
