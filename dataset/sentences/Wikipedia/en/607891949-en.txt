The Viceroyalty of the Río de la Plata was the last and most shortlived viceroyalty created by Spain in 1776. (The Spanish name, Virreinato del Río de la Plata, translates literally to Viceroyalty of the River of Silver, although some sources conventionally call the viceroyalty Viceroyalty of the River Plate; see the [http://www.britannica.com/eb/article-9109567 Encyclopædia Britannica entry].)Its limits roughly contained the territories of present Argentina, Bolivia, Paraguay and Uruguay.
