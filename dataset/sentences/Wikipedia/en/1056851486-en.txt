From 1883 to 1886 he attended the War Academy.
Until 1900 he commanded a cavalry regiment, at which point he became chief of staff of XVIII Corps.
In November 1917 he defended against the British in the Battle of Cambrai, which saw the first use of tanks en masse.
In September 1918 he took command of the Fifth Army, which he held until the end of the war.PostwarAfter the war, Marwitz withdrew from public life.
