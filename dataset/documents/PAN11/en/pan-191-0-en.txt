Finally, for some who were there, and in my opinion not really afraid,
they came and locked his arms, which had been thrashing those near
him.

Others rushed to the legs and had a hard time because there was no mule with as hard of a kick. It
took fifteen men to take him down, with hits to their nose. Also, my master was in the pulpit
on his knees, hands and eyes to the sky, carried in the divine essence, so that the plants,
noises, and voices in the church had no part of the divine contemplation.
