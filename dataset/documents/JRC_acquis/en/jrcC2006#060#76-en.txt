Case T- 397/02: Order of the Court of First Instance of 13 December 2005 — Arla Foods and Others v Commission (Regulation (EC) No 1829/2002 — Registration of a designation of origin Feta — Action for annulment — Capacity to bring proceedings — Inadmissibility) 
Order of the Court of First Instance of 13 December 2005 — Arla Foods and Others v Commission 
(Case T- 397/02) [1] 
Parties 
Applicants: Arla Foods AMBA (Viby, Denmark), Løgismose A/S (Broby, Denmark), Nordex Food A/S (Dronninglund, Denmark), Sinai Landmejeri (Broby), Andelsmejeriet Sædager (Hobro, Denmark), Søvind Mejeri (Horsens, Denmark), Steensgaard Herregårdsmejeri (Millinge, Denmark), Mejeriet Grambogård I/S (Tommerup, Denmark) and Kirkeby Cheese Export (Svendborg, Denmark), (represented by G. Lett, lawyer) 
Defendant: Commission of the European Communities (represented by H. Støvlbæk, J. Iglesias Buhigues and A.-M. Rouchaud-Joët, Agents) 
Interveners in support of the applicants: United Kingdom of Great Britain and Northern Ireland (represented by P. Ormond, Agent) 
Intervener: in support of the defendant: Hellenic Republic (represented by V. Kontolaimos, I. Chalkias and M. Tassopoulou, Agents) and Syndesmos Ellinikon Viomichanion Galaktokomikon Proïonton (SEV-GAP) (represented by N. Korogiannakis, lawyer) 
Application for 
annulment of Commission Regulation (EC) No 1829/2002 of 14 October 2002 amending the Annex to Regulation (EC) No 1107/96 with regard to the name %quot%Feta%quot% (OJ 2002 L 277 p. 10) 
Operative part of the Order 
1. The action is dismissed as inadmissible. 
2. The applicants shall bear their own costs and pay those of the Commission. 
3. The Hellenic Republic, the United Kingdom of Geat Britain and Northern Ireland and Syndesmos Ellinikon Viomichanion Galaktokomikon Proïonton (SEV-GAP) shall bear their own costs.
