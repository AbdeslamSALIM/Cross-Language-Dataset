Anacardiaceae (the cashew family) is a family of flowering plants bearing fruits that are drupes and in some cases producing urushiol, an irritant. Its 82 genera include several of economic importance. Notable plants in this family include cashew (in the type genus Anacardium), mango, poison ivy, sumac, smoke tree, and pistachio.
