Baldwin V of Hainaut (1150 &ndash; December 17 1195) was count of Hainaut (1171&ndash;1195), count of Flanders as Baldwin VIII (1191&ndash;1195) and margrave of Namur as Baldwin I (1189&ndash;1195).HistoryHe was the son of Baldwin IV, Count of Hainaut. Flanders was acquired via his marriage to Margaret I of Flanders in 1169. Namur was acquired from his mother Alice of Namur.FamilyWith Margaret, Baldwin had the following issue:Isabelle of Hainaut (Valenciennes, April 1170 &ndash; March 15, 1190, Paris), married king Philip II of FranceBaldwin VI of Hainaut (1171&ndash;1205), also count of Flanders and Latin EmperorYolanda of Flanders (1175&ndash;1219), married Peter II of Courtenay, Latin EmperorPhilip I, Marquis of Namur (1175&ndash;1212)Henry of Flanders (1176&ndash;1216), Latin EmperorSybille of Hainault (1179 &ndash; January 9, 1217), married c. 1197 Guichard IV, Sire de Beaujeu (d. 1216)Eustace of Hainault (d. 1219), regent of the Kingdom of ThessalonicaGodfrey of HainaultSee alsoCounts of Hainaut family treeCounts of Flanders family tree   Category:House of Hainaut Baldwin 8 Baldwin IV Category:Margraves of Namur Hainaut, Baldwin V, Count of Hainaut, Baldwin V, Count ofde:Balduin V.
