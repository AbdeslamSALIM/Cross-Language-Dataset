Iglesias () (pop. 29,075) is a comune of Carbonia-Iglesias province in Sardinia, Italy. Situated at 620&nbsp;ft in the hills in the southwest of Sardinia, it was a centre of a mining district, with lead, zinc, and silver being extracted, as well as for the distillation of sulfuric acid. Today 48&nbsp;% of the population of Iglesias is unemployed.Iglesias' cathedral dates from 1288, the castle from 1325; the Church of San Francesco is from the 15th century and a fine example of Gothic architecture.The municipality had a population of 28,170&nbsp;inhabitants at the 2001 census. Its land area is 207.63&nbsp;km² ().
