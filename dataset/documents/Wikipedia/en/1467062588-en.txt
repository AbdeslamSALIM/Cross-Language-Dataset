     Corn salad (Valerianella locusta) is a small dicot annual plant of the family Valerianaceae. It is also called Lewiston cornsalad, fetticus, mache, mâche, doucette, rampon, rampien, lamb's lettuce, field salad (http://de.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feldsalat), nüssli, nüsslisalat, canonigos, and rapunzel.Corn salad grows in a low rosette with spatulate leaves up to 15.2cm long.[http://www.floridata.com/ref/v/vale_loc.cfm Floridata: Valerianella locusta] It is a hardy plant that grows to zone 5, and in mild climates it is grown as a winter green. In warm conditions it tends to bolt to seed.[http://www.pfaf.org/database/plants.php?Valerianella+locusta Plants for a Future: Valerianella locusta]Corn salad grows wild in parts of Europe, northern Africa and western Asia.United States Department of Agriculture: Germplasm Resources Information Network In Europe and Asia it is a common weed in cultivated land and waste spaces. In North America it has escaped cultivation and become naturalized on both the eastern and western seaboards.[http://plants.usda.gov/java/profile?symbol=VALO United States Department of Agriculture Natural Resources Conservation Service: Plants profile for Valerianella locusta]HistoryCorn salad was originally foraged by European peasants until the royal gardener of King Louis XIV, de la Quintinie, introduced it to the world.
