Laomedeia ( ,  , or as in Greek Λαομέδεια), also known as Neptune XII, is a prograde irregular satellite of Neptune. It was discovered by Matthew J. Holman, et al. on August 13, 2002. M. Holman, JJ Kavelaars, B. Gladman, T. Grav, W. Fraser, D. Milisavljevic, P. Nicholson, J. Burns, V. Carruba, J.-M. Petit, P. Rousselot, O. Mousis, B. Marsden, R. Jacobson; Discovery of five irregular moons of Neptune, Nature, 430 (2004), pp. 865-867. Final preprint(pdf) Before the announcement of its name on February 3, 2007 (IAUC 8802), it was known as S/2002 N 3.It orbits Neptune at a distance of about 23,571,000 km and is about 42 kilometers in diameter (assuming albedo of 0.04). S. Sheppard, D. Jewitt, J. Kleyna; A Survey for "Normal" Irregular Satellites Around Neptune: Limits to Completeness, The Astronomical Journal, 132 (2006), pp. 171–176. Preprint.
