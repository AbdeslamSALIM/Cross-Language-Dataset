 York County is a county located in the U.S. state of Pennsylvania. As of 2004, the estimated population was 401,613. York County is located in the Susquehanna Valley, a large fertile agricultural region in South Central Pennsylvania.York County was created on August 19, 1749 from part of Lancaster County and named either for the Duke of York, an early patron of the Penn family, or for the city and shire of York in England. Its county seat is the city of York.GeographyAccording to the U.S. Census Bureau, the county has a total area of 910&nbsp;square miles (2,358&nbsp;km²), of which, 904&nbsp;square miles (2,343&nbsp;km²) of it is land and 6&nbsp;square miles (15&nbsp;km²) of it (0.64%) is water. The county is bound to its eastern border by the Susquehanna River.
