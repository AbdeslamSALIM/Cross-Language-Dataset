The conditions and technical requirements for issuing inland navigation certificates under Article 22 of the Revised Convention for Rhine Navigation are updated regularly and are recognised as reflecting current technological developments.
With a view to involving the co-legislators more fully in scrutinising executive acts, Council Decision 2006/512/EC of 17 July 2006[4] amending Council Decision 1999/468/EC introduced a regulatory procedure with scrutiny (Article 5a of Decision 1999/468/EC, as amended).
The new Article 19(4) stipulates that Article 5a(1), (2) and (6) and Article 7 of Council Decision 1999/468/EC are applicable, having regard to the provisions of Article 8 thereof.
The new Article 19(3) stipulates that Articles 5a(1) to (4), Article 5a(5)(b) and Article 7 of Council Decision 1999/468/EC are applicable, having regard to the provisions of Article 8 thereof.
Article 10.03 of Annex II to the Directive provides for application of the Committee procedure in respect of certain additional extinguishing agents.
