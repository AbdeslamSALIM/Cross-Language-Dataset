Specification (summary of requirements under Article 4(2)) Name : %quot%Cordero de Navarra%quot% or %quot%Nafarroako Arkumea%quot% Description : The geographical indication protects lambs of the pure-bred Navarra and Lacha breeds, marketed as fresh meat.
Ternasco lamb: male or female animal of the Navarra breed, suckled by the ewe up to a minimum of 45 days from birth, with a carcass weight of between 9 and 12 kg (not including head or offal).
Lechal lambs are suckled by ewes until they are slaughtered (25-30 days in the case of the Latxa breed and 40-45 days in the case of the Navarra breed), their carcasses reaching a weight at that time of 5 to 8 kg.
Ternasco lambs are suckled until they are at least 45 days old.
Lambs not weaned after 45 days are given the fattening diet plus their mother's milk.
At the time of slaughter, the carcasses weigh 9 to 12 kg.
