Concepts used in hydrological planning include: - The riverbed or natural course of a continuous or non-continuous flow is the land covered by water under maximum normal rises in level. - Flood plains are areas demarcated by the theoretical levels that waters would reach during flooding, the statistical recurrence interval for which could be 100 or 500 years.
