In the 1920s, Blind Lemon Jefferson innovated the style by using jazz-like improvisation and single string accompaniment on a guitar; Jefferson's influence defined the field and inspired later performers, like Lightnin' Hopkins and T-Bone Walker.
Duke Records and Peacock Records were the most important labels of the scene.In the 1960s, however, the record industry moved north, reducing Texas's importance in the blues scene.
