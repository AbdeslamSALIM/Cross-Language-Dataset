Economic activity is limited to providing services for employees of Norway's radio and meteorological stations located on the island.
It has one unpaved airstrip about 1,585&nbsp;metres (5,200&nbsp;ft) long, Jan Mayensfield, and the 124.1&nbsp;km (74.1&nbsp;mi) of coast include no ports or harbours, only offshore anchorages.There are important fishing resources outside the island, and the existence of Jan Mayen establishes a large Exclusive Economic Zone around it.
A dispute between Norway and Denmark regarding the fishing exclusion zone between Jan Mayen and Greenland was settled in 1988 granting Denmark the greater area of sovereignty.Jan Mayen is an integral part of Norway, not considered a dependency with special status.
