He completed graduate studies at Yale University, Connecticut, where he studied under James Tobin, the Nobel prize-winning economist.In 1994 he was appointed to the European Commission, along with fellow-Italian, Emma Bonino, by the first Berlusconi government.
He also led the investigation into the proposed merger between General Electric and Honeywell in 2001, which the European Commission blocked.The second Berlusconi government did not confirm him in 2004, and proposed Rocco Buttiglione in his place.
