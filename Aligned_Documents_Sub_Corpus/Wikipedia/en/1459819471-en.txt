 Greenlee County is located in the southeastern part of the U.S. state of Arizona. As of the 2000 census its population was 8,547. The county seat is Clifton. It is the least populous county in the state of Arizona, with the most populous county being Maricopa.Greenlee County is part of the Safford Micropolitan Statistical Area.HistoryGreenlee County was created in 1909 and named for Mason (Masin) Greenlee who was an early settler in the Clifton area. It was Arizona's 14th county and formed from part of Graham County, who opposed the formation because Graham County would lose considerable revenue.
