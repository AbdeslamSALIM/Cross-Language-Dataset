 | birth_place = Rome, Italy | nationality = Italian | profession = politician | party = People of Freedom Franco Frattini (born 14 March 1957 in Rome) is an Italian politician, currently serving as Italy's Foreign Minister in the new Berlusconi Cabinet. Before the 8th of May he served as European Commissioner for Justice, Freedom and Security and one of five vice-presidents of the 27-member Barroso Commission.CareerInitially a member of the Italian Socialist Party, Frattini was a member of Silvio Berlusconi's government since 2001, and Foreign Minister of Italy since November 14, 2002; he is also a member of Berlusconi's Forza Italia.On November 4, 2004, he was nominated to take up the Justice portfolio in the Commission, in place of the controversial Rocco Buttiglione (who outed him as a Freemason).[http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/news/world/article389300.ece Buttiglione 'outs' his EU successor as a Freemason &ndash; The Times, London, 10 November 2004].Retrieved 2007-07-30. His resignation from the Italian government took effect on November 18. Deputy Prime Minister Gianfranco Fini succeeded him as Foreign Minister.Reports on Gamespot showed he was seeking a Europe-wide ban on violent videogames.
