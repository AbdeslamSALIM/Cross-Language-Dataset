The Strait of Otranto connects the Adriatic Sea with the Ionian Sea. Its width is 45-55 nautical mile (85-100 km). The strait is named after the Italian city of Otranto.HistoryDuring World War I, the strait was of strategic significance. The Allied navies of Italy, France, and Great Britain, by blockading the strait, mostly with light naval forces and lightly armed fishermen, hindered the cautious Austro-Hungarian Navy from freely entering the Mediterranean Sea, and effectively kept them out of the naval theatre of war.However, the barrage was notoriously ineffective against the German u-boats operating out of the Adriatic, which were to plague the Allied powers for most of the war throughout the Mediterranean.First World War - Willmott, H.P., Dorling Kindersley, 2003, Page 186ReferencesOtranto Otranto Category:Landforms of Italy Category:Landforms of Albania bs:Otrantska vrata br:Kanol Otranto cs:Otrantský průliv de:Straße von Otranto et:Otranto väin es:Canal de Otranto fr:Canal d'Otrante gl:Estreito de Otranto ko:오트란토 해협 hi:ओत्राण्टो जलसन्धि hr:Otrantska vrata id:Selat Otranto it:Canale d'Otranto he:מצר אוטרנטו ku:Tengava Otranto lt:Otranto sąsiauris nl:Straat van Otranto new:ओत्राण्टो जलसन्धि ja:オトラント海峡 no:Otrantostredet nn:Otrantosundet pl:Cieśnina Otranto pt:Estreito de Otranto ru:Пролив Отранто sk:Otrantský prieliv sl:Otrantska vrata sr:Отрантска врата sv:Otrantosundet tr:Otranto Boğazı uk:Отранто zh:奧特朗托海峽
