He then moved across the capital to one of Ecuador's most successful sides Club Deportivo El Nacional, with whom he won successive league titles in 2005 and 2006. He again scored 11 goals in 112 matches for the club.In August 2006 he moved abroad, signing a two year deal with Serbian side Red Star Belgrade.[http://www.politika.co.yu/rubrike/Sport/t5410.lt.html Ailton i Kastiljo na "Marakani", Politika, September 1, 2006] He scored 8 goals during his first season at his new club and was a key player when the team won the double in 2007.  In total he scored 18 goals in 72 games, an average of a goal every four games.After a trial with Blackburn Rovers, in August 2008 Castillo joined English Premier League club Everton on a year-long loan deal to become Everton's second signing of the summer transfer window. In an interview, he stated his desire to win the UEFA Cup for his new club. He made his league debut on 14 September 2008 against Stoke City. He scored his first goal for the Toffees on his home debut against Standard Liege on September 18 2008 at Goodison Park.==International career==Castillo broke into the Ecuadorian national team in 2005 and made his debut against Venezuela and was included in their squad for the 2006 FIFA World Cup.
