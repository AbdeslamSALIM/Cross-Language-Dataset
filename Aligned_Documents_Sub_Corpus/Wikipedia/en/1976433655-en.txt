Cyllene ( sə-LEE-nee, or as in Greek Κυλλήνη), also known as , is a natural satellite of Jupiter. It was discovered by a team of astronomers from the University of Hawaii led by Scott S. Sheppard in 2003, receiving the temporary designation .[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/08100/08116.html IAUC 8116: Satellites of Jupiter and Saturn] 2003 April 11 (discovery)[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/mpec/K03/K03G09.html MPEC 2003-G09: S/2003 J 13] 2003 April 2 (discovery and ephemeris)Cyllene is about 2 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 23,396 Mm in 731.099 days, at an inclination of 140° to the ecliptic (140° to Jupiter's equator), in a retrograde direction and with an eccentricity of 0.4116.It was named in March 2005 after Cyllene, a naiad (stream nymph) or oread (mountain nymph) associated with Mount Kyllini, Greece.[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/08500/08502.html IAUC 8502: Satellites of Jupiter] 2005 March 30 (naming the moon) She was a daughter of Zeus (Jupiter).It belongs to the Pasiphaë group, irregular retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at distances ranging between 22.8 and 24.1 Gm, and with inclinations ranging between 144.5° and 158.3°.ReferencesCategory:Jupiter's moonsals:Cyllene (Mond) br:Cyllene (loarenn) bg:Силена (спътник) cs:Cyllene (měsíc) da:Cyllene (måne) de:Cyllene (Mond) es:Cilene (luna) fr:Cyllèné (lune) hr:Kilena (mjesec) it:Cillene (astronomia) lv:Silleneja (pavadonis) lt:Kilenė (palydovas) nl:Cyllene (maan) ja:キュレーネ (衛星) nds:Cyllene (Maand) pl:Cyllene (księżyc) pt:Cilene (satélite) simple:Cyllene (moon) sk:Cyllene (mesiac) sl:Kilena (luna) fi:Cyllene sv:Cyllene zh:木卫四十八
