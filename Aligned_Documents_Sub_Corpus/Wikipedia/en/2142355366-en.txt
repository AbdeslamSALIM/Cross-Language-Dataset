 Figeac () is a town in the Lot département in Quercy, Midi-Pyrénées, southwestern France.Figeac is commune and a sous-préfecture of the Lot département.CultureJean-François Champollion, the first translator of Egyptian hieroglyphics, was born in Figeac, where there is a Champollion Museum. On the "Place des écritures" (writings place) is a giant copy of the Rosetta stone, by Joseph Kosuth.
