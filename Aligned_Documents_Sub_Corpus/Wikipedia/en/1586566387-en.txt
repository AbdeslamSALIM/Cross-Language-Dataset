Chaldene ( kal-DEE-nee, or as in Greek Χαλδηνη ?), also known as , is a retrograde irregular satellite of Jupiter. It was discovered by a team of astronomers from the University of Hawaii led by Scott S. Sheppard, et al. in 2000, and given the temporary designation .[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/07500/07555.html IAUC 7555: Satellites of Jupiter] 2001 January 5 (discovery)[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/mpec/K01/K01A29.html MPEC 2001-A29: S/2000 J 7, S/2000 J 8, S/2000 J 9, S/2000 J 10, S/2000 J 11] 2001 January 15 (discovery and ephemeris)[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/mpec/K01/K01T59.html MPEC 2001-T59: S/2000 J 8, S/2000 J 9, S/2000 J 10] 2001 October 15 (revised ephemeris)Chaldene is about 3.8 kilometres in diameter, and orbits Jupiter at an average distance of 22,713 Mm in 699.327 days, at an inclination of 167° to the ecliptic (169° to Jupiter's equator), in a retrograde direction and with an eccentricity of 0.2916.It was named in October 2002 after Chaldene, the mother of Solymos by Zeus in Greek mythology.[http://cfa-www.harvard.edu/iauc/07900/07998.html IAUC 7998: Satellites of Jupiter] 2002 October 22 (naming the moon)It belongs to the Carme group, made up of irregular retrograde moons orbiting Jupiter at a distance ranging between 23 and 24 Gm and at an inclination of about 165°.ReferencesCategory:Jupiter's moons Category:Irregular satellitesals:Chaldene (Mond) br:Chaldene (loarenn) bg:Калдена (спътник) da:Chaldene (måne) de:Chaldene (Mond) es:Caldona (luna) fr:Chaldèné hr:Haldena (mjesec) it:Caldene (astronomia) lv:Kaldene (pavadonis) lt:Kaldenė (palydovas) nl:Chaldene (maan) ja:カルデネ (衛星) nds:Chaldene (Maand) pl:Chaldene (księżyc) pt:Caldene (satélite) simple:Chaldene (moon) sk:Chaldene (mesiac) sl:Haldena (luna) fi:Chaldene sv:Chaldene zh:木卫二十一
