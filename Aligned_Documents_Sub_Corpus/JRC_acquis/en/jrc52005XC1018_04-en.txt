Member State: Denmark 
Aid No: N 47/05 
Title: Aid for counselling concerning Natura 2000 forests 
Objective: 
The draft Administrative Order of 27.1.2005 ("the Order", not yet in force) provides that the Danish Forestry Association (Dansk Skovforening "the Association") will receive financial support for organising above-mentioned counselling to owners of forests in private property or associations of such owners. 
In § 2 of the Order the supported counselling given by the Association is limited to 930 hours of counselling on identifying and evaluating the condition of natural habitats as well as on entering voluntary agreements concerning the implementation of Natura 2000 forest plans. No other counselling activity is eligible for support. The aid is limited to actual costs of the counselling including reasonable administrative and travel costs associated with organising the counselling. The support is paid to the Association on the presentation of supporting documents concerning the counselling that has been organised. 
The above counselling is available free of charge to all private owners of forests or associations of such owners and is not limited to members of the Association 
Legal basis: 
Lov om skove, § 29.1.5. 
Forslag til bekendtgørelse af 27. januar 2005 
Budget: Ca DKR 600000 (about EUR 80600) per year 
Aid intensity or amount: 100 % of the eligible costs 
Duration: Until 31.12.2010 
The authentic text(s) of the decision, from which all confidential information has been removed, can be found at 
http://europa.eu.int/comm/secretariat_general/sgb/state_aids/ 
Date of adoption of the decision: 4.8.2005 
Member State: The Netherlands 
Aid No: N 102/2005 
Title: Compensation for avian influenza 
Objective: The purpose of the scheme is to partially compensate egg multipliers that have suffered losses because of the ban on export of hatching eggs, or because of zone restrictions imposed in connection with the outbreak of avian influenza in the Netherlands in 2003 
Legal basis: 
De Beschikkingen 2003/153/EG van 3 maart 2003, 2003/172/EG van 12 maart 2003, 2003/191/EG van 19 maart 2003, 2003/214/EG van 27 maart 2003, 2003/258/EG van 10 april 2003 en 2003/290/EG van 25 april 2003 van de Commissie (incl. verschillende wijzigingen), alle betrekking hebbende op beschermende maatregelen in verband met aviaire influenza in Nederland. 
Richtlijn 92/40/EEG van de Raad tot vaststelling van communautaire maatregelen voor de bestrijding van aviaire influenza. 
Verordening (EG) nr. 1249/2004 van de Commissie van 7 juli 2004 houdende vaststelling van buitengewone maatregelen ter ondersteuning van de markt in de sector eieren in Nederland. 
Regeling vervoersbeperkingsgebieden pluimvee 2003, geconsolideerde versie 14-08-2003 
Budget: The expenditure will amount to maximum EUR 1,5 million 
Aid intensity or amount: For chicken hatching eggs the aid constitutes approximately 27 % of the financial loss suffered. For turkey and duck eggs the compensation is approximately 9 %, for pheasant eggs 8 %. 
Duration: The scheme covers the outbreak of avian influenza in the Netherlands in 2003. Movement restriction measures were in place from 1 March 2003 to 22 August 2003. This is also the duration of the aid scheme. 
The authentic text(s) of the decision, from which all confidential information has been removed, can be found at 
http://europa.eu.int/comm/secretariat_general/sgb/state_aids/

Member State: Sweden 
Aid No: N 167/2005 
Title: Investment aid for planting multi-annual energy crops 
Objective: Aid would be granted for the investment costs regarding planting multi-annual energy crops falling under the CN code 06029041, in particular coppice (Salix ff.), used to produce biomass. The aid is limited to the planting costs of new plantations. Replacement plantations and planting measures undertaken before submitting the application for aid to the authorities are not eligible for the aid. Every applicant whose planting project fulfils the conditions for receiving aid is legally entitled to it. The aid is limited to SEK 5000 per ha planted with energy crops. According to the Swedish authorities planting costs of coppice are SEK 12955 per ha, resulting in an aid rate of ca 40 %. The Swedish authorities have indicated that the aid may not exceed 40 % of the eligible costs 
Legal basis: Åtgärden baseras på förordning (1997:75) om anläggningsstöd till plantering av fleråriga grödor för produktion av biomassa, ändrad genom regeringens beslut om att infoga det anmälda stödet 
Budget: SEK 6 million (ca EUR 655180) 
Aid intensity or amount: Max. 40 % of the eligible costs 
Duration: Until 31.12.2006 
The authentic text(s) of the decision, from which all confidential information has been removed, can be found at 
http://europa.eu.int/comm/secretariat_general/sgb/state_aids/ 
Date of adoption of the decision: 14.9.2005
