Member State : Italy 
Aid No : N 45/05 
Title : Measures to ensure food quality and the adoption of a quality label "Quality with indication of origin" 
Objective : With the draft Provincial Act on Measures to ensure food quality and the adoption of a quality label "Quality with indication of origin" the Italian authorities would introduce an aid scheme under which support could be granted for the marketing, promotion and advertising of quality products and a quality label "Quality with indication of origin". The aid scheme would apply to the following products: products satisfying the criteria laid down in Article 24a (b) of Regulation (EC) No 1257/1999, as modified by Regulation (EC) No 1783/2003, and bearing the quality label pursuant to § 3 of the draft Act; products falling under the scope of Regulation (EEC) No 2081/92, i.e. products covered either by a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) and Protected Geographical Indication (PGI) or of Regulation (EEC) No 2082/92, i.e. Traditional Speciality Guaranteed (TSG); Quality wines pursuant to Regulation (EC) No 1493/1999 
Legal basis : Misure per garantire la qualità nel settore dei prodotti alimentari e adozione del "marchio di qualità con indicazione di origine" (legge provinciale) 
Budget : Ca EUR 3 million per year 
Aid intensity or amount : Variable 
Duration : Unlimited

http://europa.eu.int/comm/secretariat_general/sgb/state_aids/

Member State : Finland 
Aid No : N 285/2004 
Title : Amendment of the rural development aid scheme N 679/2002 
Objective - When the aid rate fixed in Commission Decision C(2004) 457fin. of 16.3.2004 (case N 518/2003) exceeds 50 % of the eligible costs (or 60 % in case of young farmers) the aid in excess of these rates would be paid in annual instalments of equal amount during the five years following the completion of the investment. This amendment is in force until 31.12.2007. 
- When aid is granted at the fixed rates referred to at point 1 above as a combination of grant and subsidised loan the total funding may exceed the eligible costs. The maximum aid intensities laid down in Article 7 of the Council Regulation (EC) No 1257/1999, as amended by Council Regulation (EC) No 1783/2003 or, if applicable, the aid rates specified in Annex I to the above Decision on case N 518/2003 will be adhered to. Moreover, the amount in excess must be used towards the investment in question. This amendment is in force until 31.12.2007. 
- Eligible investment costs will be limited to those laid down in Article 4 point 4 of Commission Regulation (EC) No 1/2004. 
- If an investment project consists of several parts belonging together the aid intensity is that applicable to the main part of the investment. 
- The aid intensity of investment aid to young farmers would be raised to 60 %. 
- Technical changes would be introduced in national provision governing the notified aid (Government Decree on the development of rural industries) 
Legal basis : A government decree (not yet issued). The decree would modify the Government Decree on the development of rural industries (Valtioneuvoston asetus maaseudun kehittämisestä) 
Budget : The budget for the investment aid scheme, as amended by the present notification, has been estimated at EUR 150 million/year. The exact budgetary impact of the proposed amendment cannot be estimated yet 
Aid intensity or amount : See above under "Objective" 
Duration : Unlimited except for certain notified amendments to the existing scheme
